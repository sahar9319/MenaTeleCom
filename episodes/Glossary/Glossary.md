# MENA Telecom — Consolidated Table: Cell-by-Cell Reading Guide

A plain-language walkthrough of every cell in the Consolidated Table. For each operator and year, each metric is shown with its value as a sub-heading and a paragraph explaining what it is, how it is derived, and what you should take away. All revenue is in **USD billions**. 2024–2025 are reported actuals; 2026–2030 are analyst estimates. Legacy = voice, SMS and basic connectivity; New Services = cloud/ICT, AI, cybersecurity, IoT, fintech, enterprise digital and media/OTT.

# STC

STC Group is Saudi Arabia's largest telecom operator, majority-owned by the Public Investment Fund. FY2024 group revenue was about SAR 75.9bn (+5.7% YoY), roughly $20.2bn. Its new-services engine spans Center3 and stc cloud (data centres/cloud), sirar by stc (cybersecurity), stc pay and stc bank (fintech), Intigral/stc tv (digital media) and solutions by stc (enterprise ICT). The rows below track STC's legacy-versus-new revenue mix in USD bn for 2024–2030, with 2024–2025 reported actuals and 2026–2030 analyst estimates. STC ranks #2 of 4 on new-services share in 2024 and reaches a 50/50 split by 2027.

**New-services rank:** #2 of 4 (ranked by 2024 new-services share; the 2024 order is e& #1, STC #2, Zain #3, Ooredoo #4).


## STC — 2024  (Actual)

### Total Revenue: $20.20B
This is STC's total group revenue for 2024, $20.2 billion, and it is the anchor for the whole block — the one reported number everything else is scaled from. It was converted to USD from the reported SAR 75.9bn (+5.7% YoY), converted at the SAR/USD peg (3.75). Read it as the size of the entire business in the base year: the sum of what legacy services ($12.52B) and new services ($7.68B) brought in. Every later year's total is grown from this starting point, so if you change one figure in the model, change this one.

### Total YoY: —
There is no growth figure in the base year because the model has no 2023 to compare against, so this cell is deliberately blank. Growth rates begin in 2025. Treat 2024 purely as the starting line.

### Legacy Absolute Revenue: $12.52B
This is the dollar value of STC's legacy business in 2024 — traditional voice calls, SMS and basic 2G/3G/broadband connectivity — at $12.52B. It is calculated as total revenue × legacy share ($20.20B × 62%). Read it as the size of the 'old' business before the digital shift accelerates; it is the base that the following years grow from.

### Legacy YoY: —
No legacy growth rate is shown in the base year because there is no prior in-model year to compare against. Legacy growth rates begin in 2025.

### Legacy Share of Total: 62%
This says that in 2024, 62% of STC's revenue still came from legacy services (it is legacy ÷ total, $12.52B ÷ $20.20B). Watch this number fall as you read down the years — but remember from the legacy-dollars cell that the fall is caused by new services growing faster, not by legacy losing money. This figure and the new-services share always add up to 100%.

### New Services Absolute Revenue: $7.68B
This is the dollar value of STC's new-services business in 2024, $7.68B — cloud and ICT, AI and data, cybersecurity, IoT, fintech, enterprise digital and media/OTT (the per-operator breakdown is on the 'New Svcs Breakdown' sheet). It is simply total minus legacy ($20.20B − $12.52B). Read it as how big the 'future' business already is at the start of the forecast.

### New Services YoY: —
No new-services growth rate is shown in the base year (no prior in-model year to compare against). New-services growth begins in 2025.

### New Services Share of Total: 38%
This says new services made up 38% of STC's revenue in 2024 (it is 1 − legacy share, or new ÷ total). It rises every year as digital outgrows legacy.

### Status: Actual
'Actual' means STC's 2024 figures are anchored to reported results from annual reports and investor disclosures (shown green in the workbook). In this model, 2024 and 2025 are actuals you can take as fact.


## STC — 2025  (Actual)

### Total Revenue: $22.02B
This is STC's total group revenue for 2025, a figure of $22.02B. It ties to reported FY2025 results. It is derived in the model: it takes legacy revenue and divides by the legacy share ($12.77B ÷ 58%), which is the same as adding legacy and new services together. It is +9.0% larger than 2024's $20.20B. The takeaway: the top line keeps climbing because the fast-growing new-services side more than makes up for the slow-growing legacy side.

### Total YoY: +9.0%
This is how fast STC's total revenue grew from 2024 to 2025: +9.0%, taking it from $20.20B to $22.02B. It is a blended number — a weighted mix of the slow legacy growth (+2.0%) and the fast new-services growth (+20.5%) on the same row. The takeaway: even though legacy barely grows, the overall company still posts strong high-single-digit to low-double-digit growth because new services pull the average up.

### Legacy Absolute Revenue: $12.77B
This is STC's legacy revenue in 2025, $12.77B, grown from 2024's $12.52B. The single most important point in the whole table lives here: legacy revenue is still **rising in absolute dollars** (+2.0%) even though its share of the mix is falling to 58%. In other words, the legacy business is not shrinking — it is growing slowly while the rest grows faster. The 'decline' people talk about is only relative, not a loss of dollars.

### Legacy YoY: +2.0%
This is how much STC's legacy revenue grew this year: +2.0%. It is the one editable assumption that drives the legacy dollars, set deliberately low but **positive** to reflect a mature voice/SMS/connectivity base that still expands a little each year rather than collapsing. Compare it with the new-services growth on the same row (+20.5%): legacy grows a fraction as fast, which is precisely why its share of the pie keeps slipping while its dollar total keeps inching up.

### Legacy Share of Total: 58%
This says that in 2025, 58% of STC's revenue still came from legacy services (it is legacy ÷ total, $12.77B ÷ $22.02B). Watch this number fall as you read down the years — but remember from the legacy-dollars cell that the fall is caused by new services growing faster, not by legacy losing money. This figure and the new-services share always add up to 100%.

### New Services Absolute Revenue: $9.25B
This is STC's new-services revenue in 2025, $9.25B (total minus legacy), up +20.5% from 2024's $7.68B. This is the fastest-growing part of the business and the real engine behind the rising total. The takeaway: the dollars here are climbing roughly ten times faster than legacy, which is what reshapes the revenue mix over the decade.

### New Services YoY: +20.5%
This is how fast STC's new-services revenue grew this year: +20.5%, from $7.68B to $9.25B. Set it next to the legacy growth on the same row (+2.0%) and the whole story snaps into focus — new services are growing many times faster, which is exactly why the new-services share keeps climbing toward 42%.

### New Services Share of Total: 42%
This says new services made up 42% of STC's revenue in 2025 (it is 1 − legacy share, or new ÷ total). It rises every year as digital outgrows legacy.

### Status: Actual
'Actual' means STC's 2025 figures are anchored to reported results from annual reports and investor disclosures (shown green in the workbook). In this model, 2024 and 2025 are actuals you can take as fact.
